## Project Overview
This Project focuses on analysing the Solar Reference Access 2035 – Moderate Supply
curve dataset using Excel & Power BI. The dataset contains information about solar
resource potential, land availability, installed capacity, generation cost (LCOE –
Levelized Cost of energy), transmission cost, and total energy cost across various
locations.

## Data Sourcce
**Source Description:**
- https://data.openei.org/files/8319/solar_reference_access_2035_moderate_supply_curve.csv
- Supply Curve Type: Moderate
- Time Horizon: Projection for year 2035
- Domain: Renewable Energy

## Data Cleaning & Preparation
The raw dataset was processed to ensure accurate and reliable analysis
- Removed blank rows and duplicate entries
- Checked and corrected data types (numeric, text, decimal)
- Converted capacity factor values into percentage format
- Verified missing or inconsistent values
- Created pivot tables in Excel for preliminary analysis
- Imported the cleaned dataset into Power BI for visualization

## Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Several exploratory analyses were performed to understand the data patterns and relationships.
**Key questions explored include:**
- Which states have the highest installed solar capacity?
- How does solar resource availability vary by state?
- What is the average total energy cost across regions?
- How does transmission cost influence overall energy cost?
- Which sites have the lowest total energy cost for potential investment?

Power BI visualizations such as bar charts, column charts, scatter plots, KPI cards, and tables were used to explore these questions.

## Key Insights
- The average solar resource across the analyzed states is 3.51 kWh/m²/day, indicating moderate solar energy potential
- Washington and Montana show higher installed capacity and lower energy cost compared to other states
- Maine has the highest total energy cost, mainly due to high transmission and grid reinforcement expenses
- Transmission distance and infrastructure significantly influence the overall energy cost of solar projects
- Regions with lower transmission cost and stable solar resource offer better investment opportunities for solar development

## Recommendations:
Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are suggested:
- Prioritize solar development in regions with lower transmission cost and strong solar resource availability.
- Improve grid infrastructure in high-cost states to reduce transmission expenses.
- Focus investment on low-cost sites identified in the Top-10 lowest cost analysis.
- By 2035, moderate supply curve scenario suggests cost stabilization in regions
  with strong grid connectivity.
